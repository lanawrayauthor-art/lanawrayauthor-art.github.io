## Description (4000 char max)
---
Every manager dreads the hard conversations. The performance review that keeps getting postponed. The termination you've rehearsed in your head a dozen times. The salary discussion where the answer is no. The Slack message you've rewritten six times because you can't get the tone right.

This book gives you the exact words — and the science behind them.

Drawing on neuroscience research from Porges, Rock, Lieberman, Eisenberger, and Bailenson, this practical guide explains what happens in the brain during difficult conversations and gives you evidence-based strategies to navigate them with both clarity and compassion.

INSIDE YOU'LL FIND:

• The CLEAR Framework — a universal 5-step structure (Calm, Listen, Empathize, Assert, Resolve) you can apply to any difficult conversation

• 11 word-for-word scripts for the most common management challenges: performance improvement, negative feedback, attendance issues, salary disappointment, termination, team conflict, and burnout check-ins

• 4 scripts designed specifically for remote and hybrid teams: difficult Slack messages, video 1:1s, written performance reviews, and camera-off culture interventions

• Neuroscience explainers with every script — understand why people get defensive, shut down, or fight back, and how to work with their brain instead of against it

• Comparison tables showing what changes neurologically between in-person, video, and async conversations

• A follow-up framework with specific cadences for each conversation type

• A quick reference card with power phrases, common mistakes, and a conversation decision guide

This is not a book of theory. It's a toolkit. Each script tells you exactly what to say, what NOT to say, what's happening in the other person's brain, and what to do after the conversation ends.

Whether you're a new manager having your first tough conversation or an experienced leader looking for better frameworks, this guide will help you stop avoiding and start leading.

For every manager who chose courage over comfort.

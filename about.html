About the Author

Lana Wray

I spent over a decade in corporate HR — from startups to publicly traded companies, from building teams of 10 to restructuring organizations of thousands. I've been a Chief Human Resources Officer, an HR Business Partner at senior director level, and the person everyone came to when things got hard.

I also burned out. Badly.

When I finally stopped long enough to understand what had happened to me, I discovered that burnout isn't a motivation problem — it's a neurological one. My background in psychology and neurobiology helped me see the patterns: the HPA axis dysregulation, the chronic cortisol exposure, the prefrontal cortex going offline.

That discovery became the foundation for everything I build now.

The Methodology

My work sits at the intersection of neuroscience, corporate culture, and human performance. Every book, tool, and assessment I create is grounded in peer-reviewed research — from the HPA axis and cortisol biology to prefrontal cortex function and vagus nerve regulation.

The REMOTE Stress Model — measuring Role Blur, Empathy Depletion, Micro-Surveillance, Osmotic Loneliness, Techno-Overload, and Escapelessness — is the first neuroscience-based burnout framework designed specifically for the remote workforce. Over 2,000+ professionals have taken the free assessment.

I write for people who have given too much to organizations that took too much — and who are ready to understand their brains and rebuild.
